StoryCare changes that. It turns what someone shares into images, music, and film they keep — co-created with their therapist, built from their own words.

The Story Page

Images, music, and reflection. Generated from the session.

Clinical Framework

Grounded in evidence. Structured for scale.

StoryCare delivers Digital Narrative Exposure Therapy (DNET), a structured protocol drawing on Narrative Exposure Therapy, Narrative-Enactment and Creative Therapy, and Yalom's group therapeutic factors. The default protocol runs 8 modules over roughly 4 months, but the platform adapts to shorter or longer engagements, individual or group formats, and a range of clinical populations.

It's transdiagnostic: PTSD, schizophrenia-spectrum conditions, substance use disorders, depression, and anxiety. The digital layer records, transcribes, generates media, and captures outcomes at every step.

  • 02 Identity Anchoring
  • 03 Narrative Exposure I

No. The therapist runs the session, reviews the transcript, selects what matters, and decides what the platform creates. StoryCare is a tool in the clinician's hands, not a substitute for clinical judgment.
StoryCare is HIPAA-compliant with end-to-end encryption, role-based access, and audit logging. Audio, transcripts, and generated media are stored securely and never used to train AI models.
StoryCare is currently in clinical evaluation and available to clinicians and researchers interested in early adoption. It's designed for residential, outpatient, and community settings working with group or individual therapy.
The patient receives a Story Page on their phone with a short video, original music, images, reflection prompts, and survey questions. Everything is generated from what they shared in session and curated by their therapist.
StoryCare was built around Digital Narrative Exposure Therapy (DNET), but the core tools — transcription, media generation, Story Pages, and outcome tracking — can support other structured therapeutic approaches.
